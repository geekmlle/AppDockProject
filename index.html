AppDock

An Education and Outreach Space for Mobile Literacy in Developing Countries

What is AppDock?

Problem

The number of mobile phone subscriptions exceeds 650 million in Africa. However, the usage of mobile apps and services (especially the locally developed ones) remains low across the continent. This is unfortunate because they could provide critical support to the public in sectors ranging from health and education to agriculture.

Where is the disconnect? Reasons for limited app usage include low literacy and digital literacy rate, unfamiliarity with the notion of app and app store, misalignment between apps and users’ needs, and inadequate access and support infrastructure.

Solution: AppDock

AppDock is an outreach model with physical and digital components that aims at addressing the key issues that impede mobile literacy in developing markets. It was designed collaboratively by architecture and computer science researchers and students from NYIT and Pace University respectively.

AppDock is an eco-friendly and community-based physical space, no larger than a bus stop, where local developers and end users can connect and learn. It is also a set of bike-driven stalls for remote outreach.

When visiting AppDock, the public can charge phones and access hotspots, watch video tutorials, sample locally developed apps, and provide feedback to developers using secured devices.

Local developers can promote their apps, gather feedback from users about existing apps, and interview them about needs and app ideas via focus groups and interactive surveys.

Inspiration and New Directions

AppDock is inspired by the “Digital Drum” and the “Hole in the Wall” projects in Uganda and India respectively. It takes the concept of publicly available computers to support digital literacy to new directions by focusing on mobile devices and considering the perspective of the local developers.

Value Proposition

AppDock prepares users for the digital world through devices that they are more likely to have access to. It permits reciprocity of interaction between users and developers and democratizes app development.

Next Steps

We are planning to test AppDock in Little Senegal in Harlem during the Summer 2016 to gather initial feedback. We will then integrate the feedback to build the first AppDock in Senegal.
